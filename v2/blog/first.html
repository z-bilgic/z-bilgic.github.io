Building a Website

June 2026 •

I created my first website during the summer of 2020. I had just learned the basics of HTML and CSS from Khan Academy, and spent many hours playing around on Replit.com (Repl.it at the time), and I was fascinated by the fact that the things I wrote down on my own computer could be seen by my friends on their own devices. I started with little descriptions of my birds, then went on to a small squirrel minigame.

Last year in the summer of 2025, I decided to code a new website from scratch, but this time it would be one that was actually about me. I remember trying to use the command line to commit and push my changes in VS Code to GitHub and then waiting a short while while the changes synced to the rest of the website. I (and occaisionally my father) would go on W3Schools to look up different CSS properties that we could mess around with, and eventually I finished it.

It was a really fun experience, and I learned a lot about the art of reserach and its application into the production of a website. However, I always knew that there was so much more that I could do with the website to make it look nicer.

So, thanks to the Northwestern University AI Bootcame program that I learned about through my schools Women in STEM program, I got the chance to recreate my website using a different technique: vibe coding.

It was an interesting experience, and definitely went smoother than trying to code everything by hand. Yet it also lacked the magic of creating everything from scratch. It was like using pre made brownie mix instead of combining all of the ingredients separately. While the final product was more foolproof, it also felt less personal.

I will never forget the times I spent hand coding little websites to laugh over with my friends during COVID, but I also appreciate the ease of vibe coding and the doors it opens.
